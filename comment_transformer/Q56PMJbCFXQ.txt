Depending on specific designs, fatigue cracks started to appear within a few years.  Over time, more cracks appeared, some in critical areas.  In 1991, the USCG issued a requirement that tankers involved in the TAPS trade develop a critical areas inspection plan.  The plan required identification of critical areas, usually done using finite element analysis, annual inspections of the critical areas, development of approved repairs, and record keeping of cracks and repairs made.  Rules for the design and construction of tankers evolved from the cracks found in tankers in the TAPS trade.
