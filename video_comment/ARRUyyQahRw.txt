I love how every hero in Rivals is "broken".  LoL.  Seriously, go to any stream on Twitch and you'll get nothing but completley opposite opinions on the same hero.  Why?  Because there's enough variance and nuance in each hero that the top strategies for "how they should be played" haven't been worked out yet--and, even if they had, there's always someone with a different strategy that no one else has thought of--not to mention map and complementary hero synergies and team compositions.  The fundamental error a lot of people are making is that Rivals is the same game as Overwatch: it isn't.  For example:  Depending on who you talk to, Punisher is either "god tier" or "trash tier" or "mid tier"--lol, seriously--but it all depends on the map and who's playing him, and whether he has a the "right" tank and support in his team.  According to a lot of newer players, Iron Fist is "trash tier", but if you play him in a particular way, utilizing his verticality, he absolutely dominates as a back-line pounce predator.  The same can be said for almost every hero:  That's the cool thing about Rivals.  There's none of this nerfed-into-the-ground-because-of-meta-reasons B.S. you have with Overwatch, so heroes can be played in all kinds of interesting ways that are not hamstrung by the "no fun police".    All the heroes in Rivals have less dependency on role-specific meta strategy because they're all designed to be a little bit more self-sustaining than the heroes in Overwatch, which means there's interesting overlap across heroes, making any pigeon-holing virtually impossible.  The result is that everyone's opinion is all over the place.
