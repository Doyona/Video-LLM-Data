General advice for wrangling non-flying mobs into a small space: drop them in from above. I know of two good ways to accomplish this (and have used both successfully):   1. Construct a water stream that flows to an opening above the space that you want the mob to end up in and use their path-finding to get them into the water. The rest should take care of itself (although you may need to leave their line-of-sight to get them to stop trying to path toward you against the current). Just be careful not to drop them from too high up. (Tip: signs or opened fence-gates can be used to make the x/z position above the hole water-free, regardless of how far away the water source is).  2. Create a "corridor" that passes over a hole above the space you want the mob to end up in with open trapdoor(s) above the hole, spanning the corridor's width (make sure the space with the open trapdoors is large enough for the mob to fit through). Get the mob to follow you down the corridor and when you reach the trapdoors jump over them to the far side; the mob should walk right into the hole (since mobs treat trapdoors as through they can walk on them regardless of whether or not they're open).

Hey Scar. I have a huge aesthetic tip for your Create Mod house. You can hide the minecart and rails for your house by putting them underground and connecting the cart contraption assembler to your house with a glass pane column. Much more inconspicuous. You will need the space where the glass comes to a rest, while the house is stationary, to be open, but that would only be a one-by-one hole in your garage house. Also, if you don't know yet, if you place a powered rail under the assembler and leave each rail on either side as regular ones, the minecart will stop when the house does.
