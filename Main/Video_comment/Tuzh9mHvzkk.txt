It's kind of cool to see someone actually trying to use a tesla turbine. I used to be really enamored with the idea of the turbine, mainly because it was so different. The reason the design never caught on is that typical reaction turbines were already really efficient by the time you could actually manufacture a tesla turbine properly. A typical steam turbine from a power plant is somewhere in the range of 80-90%, to my knowledge, though I've heard some people quote higher numbers. I think the biggest issue with a tesla turbine is actually torque. The guy said that the tesla turbine makes the most torque the greater the velocity difference between the turbine discs and the incoming fluid. The issue with that is that a tesla turbine should be hitting peak efficiency when the velocity of the fluid is as close as possible to the velocity of the turbine, which would also be the point where the turbine has its least torque.
