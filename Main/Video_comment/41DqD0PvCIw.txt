Although music taste is subjective (I understand that the  imagine Dragon‚Äôs Ft. is probably at the heart of the controversy lol) the choice to use modern music is a very specific decision as it‚Äôs actually an age old film technique.  Here‚Äôs the TL;DR: Riot used Modern music as a tool for the audience to more easily grasp the characters feelings and the vibes/atmosphere of any given scene.     While yes Riot has a history of attaching big artists to their products, the use of modern music is used primarily for the audience benefit as it translates the tone/purpose of a scene from an otherwise fantasy world, through a modern lens making it more relatable.  For examples the director Bazza (directed Great Gatsby, Romeo and Juliet with DiCaprio, Moulin Rouge) is famous for using modern pop/rap music. In Gatsby, he uses Jay Z in a 1930s underground bar to convey the popularity, vibes, and overall atmosphere of prohibition New York. As an audience, we can relate to the characters feelings in the scene through the music alone. Even though they are in an an otherwise foreign setting, the music and the cultural/social context behind said music incites the proper emotions from the audience.  Same thing with Moulin rouge which takes place in 1920s France. Instead of the characters singing an era/regional appropriate love song, they sing Elton John. Again to convey the emotions and vibes through a modern lens, something we as an audience understand and relate to more.  This is what arcane does, they use modern songs and artists to get their point across. The opening song ‚ÄòWelcome to the playground‚Äô is very much a modern pop grunge song (Billie Ellish-esque).  We can recognize it as unsettling, underground, slightly creepy, it has a dark but fantastical quality to it. That‚Äôs exactly the vibes the creators want us to feel when Vi and the gangs first enter the Undercity. It‚Äôs  dangerous, slightly fantastical, absurd, grungy, steam punk.   We sometimes take for granted the cultural contexts and clues that comes with music. The nuances and intricacies of say old timey music would go straight over our head. Vi walks into a night club and they play royalty free generic fantasy-genre music, no one bats an eye. But when Vi walks into a nightclub and The Weeknd‚Äôs Hills is blasting, the mood is instantly set. That‚Äôs also the benefit of using big name artists, just another layer of cultural context and lens we subconsciously relate too. Music when done right is a powerful tool film makers use to get the audience immersed into the world and to buy into these characters and their emotions. Modern music bridges that gap of ambiguity between the fictional setting of the film and the real world by relying on the cultural connotations of the music to covey the proper atmosphere and tone.
