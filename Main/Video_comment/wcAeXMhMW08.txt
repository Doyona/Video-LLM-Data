A group of friends and I went on a backpacking trip to the west coast of British Columbia in early June. Part of me honestly thought we would run into you two.   So, since on this trip you're come into my world let me give you some tips. 1- You're working way too hard with that JetBoil. Fully assemble it - fuel, burner, and pot - then pour in water. Well, unless you've gotten water from the creek/lake in the pot, then remove it from the stove before dipping the water. Regardless, the filled pot works best if attached to the stove when lighting. And put the lid on. The water will boil quicker.  And yes, I said unfiltered water. I seldom filter the water I cook with. 30 seconds of boiling will make water safe to drink anywhere in the contiguous US unless the water had harmful toxins in it.  2- I take a "dirty water" bottle on trips. Then I don't have to take a second trip back to the water source in the morning to make breakfast. All my water for the day got filtered the night before and I'm ready to go.   3- Nut butter packets. They're way lighter and less bulky than a jar. Get them at the grocery store. We also do flavored tuna packs on tortillas for lunch. So good.  4- Trekking poles.
