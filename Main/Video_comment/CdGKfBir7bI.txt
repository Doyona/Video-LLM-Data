Some rambling thoughts on the finale:  Watching countless Ricks and Mortys die in The Rickshank Redemption was sad but also comedic. Watching the Rick and Morty massacre in this episode was genuinely horrific. Incredible episode.  It introduces a (now confirmed) layer to the quote "Ricks hate themselves the most," seeing how a Rick likely killed C-137's family. Imagine have to stare at the face of your family's killer in the mirror over and over again (the extreme guilt!) and trying to get revenge on said killer over and over again- failing each time- having only more fakes to hunt down, face, and fail. That is self-loathing taken to the extreme.  I know Ricks are considerred evil but there had to have been heroic Ricks trying to save other more innocent Ricks from C-137's rampage only to die to his wrath and eventual callousness. Watching C-137 going around killing Ricks who were just minding their business was horrific as well. The image of a Rick surviving the carnage and begging at C-137's leg for mercy alongside another Rick basically submitting to C-137 as though C-137 were a God was particularly chilling. You don't see Ricks that helpless. Reminds me of when C-137 prayed to God near death.  There's also no guarantee all Ricks with portal technology were evil and abandoned their families. Remember Evil Rick's ranking of the morality of Ricks? I keep thinking the Beth the show started with had a Rick killed by C-137's rampage, which is why she thought her father abandoned her. He could have been adventuring or been trying to take down the murderer C-137 was proving himself to be. Said deceased Rick could have been a man of family AND science- not including all the other, healthier, Ricks who may have managed to balance their scientific-portal-life with their personal life. What if there were Ricks adventuring to find a way to save their loved ones from something that wasn't their doing only to get killed by C-137? There were so many Ricks killed who could have been honorable figures and those who never abandoned their families but were taken away from them. Imagine that being further explored as a plot point.   The Rick that likely killed C-137's family? What if he was the one who wanted to kill a bunch of Ricks and didn't mistakenly miss killing C-137 but used C-137 as a pawn to carry out Rick genocide? What if that killer Rick would later have Evil Morty as a grandson/sidekick- even if temporarily? That would help explain both of their genocidal tendencies for their own goals especially if their connection was genetic (or if Evil Morty adopted similar behaviors as said Rick).  Speaking of, what if Evil Morty was passed along from one unspeakably evil or insane Rick to the next? Or instead of being passed on, Evil Morty kept fleeing on his own only to be captured by other horrible Ricks in a nearly endless cycle? That would also help in explaining his obsession with fleeing absolutely everything Rick or Morty.  Honestly I'm a tiny bit disappointed that Rick's backstory was 80% accurate. I was hoping for something like <50% accurate. I mean to say, snippets of Rick's backstory could have truths or lead to other truths, I felt like we were given too much information and the writers should have committed to more of the falsehood, which would make the later reveal be more shocking/new. I feel like a majority of the backstory being true doesn't match up with the quote "totally fabricated origin story" quote- I know Rick's trolling but it made me hyped for a bigger mystery. One example of what I was feeling; I was hoping the name "Diane" was the name of an important canon figure but not actually the name of Rick's wife in the way the audience was led to believe. Or that he lost his family a different way. So not a total lie, not the total truth- just slightly misleading. But what the writers did with Rick's reaction and descent was very fascinating so props to that! I'm really just nitpicking the result was still very cool and I enjoyed the episode immensely.  I also kinda wished Evil Morty wasn't written to have lied to Morty about the escape seat, it would have been interesting to see a single Morty that evil Morty doesn't entirely despise. That type of villainous betrayal isn't that fun. But Rick's line afterwards was definitely funny. But Evil Morty being so emotionally detached is also very interesting in its own right so this is also obvious nitpicking.  Since green portals are theorized to connect to only other universes where a Rick is the most intelligent, it appears the earlier portal-gun-inventor-Ricks designed them this way from the start. They never planned to be able to travel to non-Rick-dominated worlds. Unless they did but someone came by and changed things to prevent more Ricks from getting killed too early.   I wonder how the comic writers are going to deal with this new canon. How are they going to work with their established Citadel of Mortys and role-swapped Rick and Mortys? How does the the comic, Rick and Morty Presents: The Council of Ricks, fit into the new canon? I know the show is more canon than the comics but the poor comic writers have to adjust to all these other revelations in their own works. The Central Finite Curve's purpose suggests Tall Morty/Slow Rick used to be intelligent or that he's somehow a type of Morty- or whatever other fun reason. I suppose it can be handwaved by the explanation that the comics are focusing on an alternate Central Finite Curve nearly identical to the one in the show, since an infinite number of realities mean an infinite number of intelligent Rick's to hand into an infinite number of Finite Curves.  Rick and Morty's relationship is very interesting here (I feel a certain subtext going on) it's definitely cool to watch how the two deal with eachother as they're clearly codependent in a way that started out rather similar to Beth and Jerry- which Jerry even pointed out.  I, like countless others, really wished we saw more of the citadel and everyday life there. So many opportunities for more jokes. And characters too. Honestly I felt kind of emotional for the loss of the Citadel alongside its Ricks and Mortys. Got emotional at the breakup and even Evil Morty finally becoming free. Still mourning, not going to lie.
