Wool is the only block that does not produce a sound that objects and mobs of the deep dark can hear. It's a mechanic called "wool occlusion." Your best bet if you spawn a Warden is to, like I said earlier, get to high ground fast, but silently (i.e. nothing but crouching and do not place blocks other than wool) after you get up, place wool around you so you have it around you on three sides and a wall on the fourth side. Wait for the Warden to walk up to you (it can smell you) and dig carefully into the wall. The wool should stop the sound and allow you to dig without the wardens sonic attack going through it and killing you. Just get about 20 blocks away (or mine until the darkness effect clears and mine about four or five blocks further) and wait silently. The Warden will lose track of you and eventually despawn in a minute or so. As for the blocks that spawn the Warden; they are called Sculk Shriekers and only trigger if a sculk sensor (the blocks you found first that made noise when you placed a torch in this video) activates within a certain range (I do not specifically remember the exact range) or if you interact in any way with the inside of the top of the block. If you stand on it, it will trigger, if an arrow from a bow hits it, it triggers, if an item hits it, it will trigger. Your best option is to either cover these blocks in wool, or break them and make sure the sound wont trigger other shriekers via the sensors.
