Good coverage of the Princess experience. Here are a few things that weren't mentioned: Cunard is the only line, to my knowledge, that maintains the class system. The lowest class is Britannia, for which the main restaurant is the Britannia Grill. The menu choices in this restaurant are much more limited than in the higher classes, Princess Grill and Queen's Grill, there is no a la carte menu, and each course is limited to a choice of four or five items, one of which is vegetarian or vegan and one "healthy". Britannia passengers can alternately eat at specialty restaurants for an additional charge, or at the pub or buffets. During off season fare in Britannia class can be under $1000.  The top class, Queen's Grill, is Princess on steroids. Large cabins and suites, extensive choices in the restaurant including caviar, and your choice of free alcoholic beverages in your cabin, replenished as needed. Afternoon tea and cocktails in the area reserved for Princess and Queen passengers only. Two assigned butlers per cabin. One crossing on the Queen Victoria I was upgraded to the enormous Berengaria Suite with two bathrooms, living and dining rooms, tub and shower, kitchen, and enormous wrap-around deck.
