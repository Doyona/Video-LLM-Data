The subsequent graves of the family are buried in circles around the center. Theodore Sedgwick, along with Tapping Reeve of CT, ended slavery in MA when a local woman who came to be known as Elizabeth Freeman (or Mum Bett), upon hearing the reading of the newly passed state constitution and the words "all men are created equal" hired him and Reeve to petition for her freedom in court. They won, she was freed, and the precedent ended slavery in the state. She went on to live with the Sedgwick family for the rest of her life as a hired servant and nanny and is buried in the first row of graves around the pie. Other famous family members include Edie Sedgwick (of Andy Warhol fame) and Kyra Sedgwick, as well as many noted writers, war heroes and professors.
