The thing about a larger workshop is setting it up with work stations. Large part forging station with a large forge, power hammer, press, and anvil. Small part forge station with a smaller forge to use less gas to heat smaller parts, a smaller power hammer, and another anvil. Hammer/tong rack between the two stations if you don't want to double the amount of tongs and hammers you have. A machining station for the machining tools. Add a painting/staining station, glue station, wood work station, general purpose workbenches central to the other stations.. and large shops starts getting small quickly.  But setting the shop up into dedicated work stations is vastly superior to having no stations. Glue station 1 workbench, rack for clamps, shelf for clamp blocks, shelf with all your glues. Workbench has a butcher paper roll on one side and a way to clamp the paper down that's not tape so your workbench can quickly have a glue covered surface removed and replaced with fresh paper. Painting station should have a workbench for doing brush on painting and stain work, a shelf with stains and paints, all your brushes sponges, and cloths. Then make a 4x6x8 foot paint booth out of galvanized fence posts and hardware that's covered in plastic sheeting, vent with a filtered vent and clothes drier exhaust tube that can be ran out the shop door to vent outside and rolled up when not needed. Put a clothes rack(on casters) inside with metal clothes hangers that were cut into hooks to hang parts to be painted, and a shelf with all your spray paints. General purpose workbenches, 1 for sanding and filing work, 1 for other uses like electrical wiring... all can be used for anything but job specific tools kept at one workbench rather than at all so your not hunting for a file or wrench.  Setting up dedicated workstations cuts down on walking in the shop, need to glue up handle scales? You go to the gluing station and everything you need to do the job is right there, no glue on a shelf in the back of the shop, clamps over in one corner, and workbench is in another... no you reach to the shelf and grab what you need right there without leaving the workbench. Then the glued up part isn't taking up space at your workbench while it dries when you want to file and sand another part... which you had all your files and sandpaper right there within reach ready to use at the sanding/filing workbench.
