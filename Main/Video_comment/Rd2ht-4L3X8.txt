ORB 3-D for NES was really good. It was an esoteric puzzle game.   You fly a ship through targeted tiles staggered on a gridded screen, passing between the foreground and the background,  similar to using the gate on the racoon fencing in Super Mario World.   You cannot fly out of the edges of the screen or you will be given a fuel penalty. When you are out of fuel you are finished.  If you miss-hit a target, you either deflect off-course or get a fuel penalty. You earn fuel by making trips to Vern's.   ORB 3-D is a pong-style riddle solving game. It is unbelievably well done, and some of the symbolism in the game are worth taking notes.   ORB 3-D was very well done, but I can't imagine it would be enjoyable to a wide audience. The game is stressful because of the fact that you are always in motion (like Pong) and you must keep within the playing field (grid) at all times while hitting riddles/targets in a particular sequence.   The game took patience and would probably drive certain people nuts.  I highly recommend ORB 3-D. I rank it highly as one of the best unknown games out there. Most people have never heard of it. I don't think they produced that many cartridges. I forget the publisher/developer.   Not only would I say ORB 3-D is a great lost title, but I would also say that it was one of the greatest games available for NES.   ORB 3-D thinks outside of the box and I rate it highly with a near perfect score.

Ducktales 2 was actually pretty popular in post-Soviet countries in 90s due to influx of cheap Famicom clones from Taiwan and various NES games on counterfeit cartridges. Back in the day, those pirates were not limited by regions and game release order - best and more fun games (that sold well) received local cult status, and some early games that defined NES library in the West and Japan stayed mostly unknown. Darkwing Duck, Chip and Dale 2, Robocop 3, Contra Force (yeah it's not the "real Contra" in the same way as Super Mario Bros 2 not "real Mario"), Mighty Final Fight, Bucky O'Hare - all were overshadowed by SNES release, but became popular on developed markets. A very wonderful NES game a lot don't talk about is 'The Guardian Legend.' Its graphics are great, its music in incredible and action packed. And the Gameplay is Unique. Its like a mix of Genres! You have the Main Stages (Corridors) that are top down shooters, you have the top down Adventure areas (Fields) that are like Legend of Zelda, and it has status, weapon, upgrades and EXP like RPGs.  Your goal is to clear each Corridor on a planet taken over by an evil race so that you can cause the planet's self destruct program. On the adventure field you wonder around, looking for upgrades, new weapons and level up by finding level ups or reaching milestones in your score, so you can hold more chips (Your MP/Currency) as well as have more HP for the harder stages. You also look for the corridors there too. (Sometimes you gotta solve a puzzle to get it to open. The clues are somewhere on the field.) When you enter the corridors, you change into a ship and fly. (Yes, your character transform into a ship. Lol. She's an android. It has a Super Sentai style to it.) Shoot enemies and reach the end till you reach and defeat the boss. You would also earn keys for completing the corridors of an area to reach the next area.  The gameplay is really fun and I highly recommend it! The only downside that I can think of is even though the game has a lengthy game time, it uses a Password system to save your progress. Other then that, its a under-rated classic! I say give it a go if you can!  Developed by Compile, who sadly isn't around anymore. So its likely that the game will never see a re-release.
