Levels of Chess is actually a tricky subject when trying to correlate it with ELO ratings. This is because a human player with ELO of 2000 doesn't exactly play like a 2000-rated computer. Computers usually make moves that are consistent to their configured ELO rating. Whereas amateurs make book/good moves in some moves, then play badly/like a beginner in other moves especially during the middlegame and endgame phase. Same can be said for tournament players. At the start of the tournament, they start really well during the first few rounds, tend to play poorly during the middle rounds and then probably bounce back on the last round when there are prizes or awards at stake. And the metric of this kind of behavior is measured in terms of the so-called Tournament Performance Rating (TPR). This means that while a computer's playing strength is defined by the parameters set to them, the playing strength of humans is defined by the average of their physical and mental performance at any given time.
