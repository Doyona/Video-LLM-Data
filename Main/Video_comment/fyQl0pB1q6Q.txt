My opinion on Stage 20, and maybe it's just me, but I'll post it here, anyway.  Roglic and Pogacar seemed evently matched. Pogacar only beat Roglic in the Slovenian TT championships by 9 seconds. So, there was an expectation that this TT would end up similarly since they were both good time trialists and both good climbers, so neither really seemed to have any significant advantage over the other.  Van Aert seemed to be doing a practice run for Jumbo's strategy. He started slow then picked it up. He was 20-30 seconds behind Cavagna's time at the first two checks, but then turned on the gas and beat Cavagna's time by almost 30 seconds. Dumoulin then did something similar and beat Van Aert by 10 seconds. So, Roglic starting out slow seemed planned. He was likely riding conservatively because he knew he had 57 seconds to play with.  Jumbo-Visma probably expected Pogacar to employ one of two strategies. Either 1) Start slower and build up so he could nail the climb - that way he wouldn't blow up on the climb and at least win the polka-dot jersey, or 2) Go all out from the beginning and then likely blow up on the climb. Either way, - since he started after Pogacar - Roglic would know all of Pogacar's time splits, so he could start slow to conserve energy and then speed up if he had to, and then nail the final climb and keep Pogacar close and still hold onto the jersey even if he lost 30 or 40 seconds. Knowing they were pretty evenly matced. they likely expected that if Pogacar went all-out from the beginning, he would probably blow up on the climb. And then since Roglic would conserve energy on the flat portion, Roglic would then have the energy to drill the climb and put time into Pogacar after Pogacar blew up on the climb from using up all his energy on the first half of the TT.  Pogacar indeed employed the second strategy. Go all-out from the beginning. So, Jumbo-Visma likely expected him to blow up. And even tho Roglic was losing time at the beginning, the gap seemed to stabilize for a while around 28 seconds which seemed to prove that they had planned it right.  However, the flaw in the plan was that Pogacar never blew up. Instead, he drilled that climb like nobody's business. And since Roglic was riding conservatively, suddenly he was on the climb and had too much time to take back. The plan was for him to lose some time in the beginning and then hold it or gain it back at the end as Pogacar tired out. But, Pogacar's didn't tire out. He got stronger and faster. His performance was so incredible that by the time Roglic reached the final climb it was too late.  Roglic would've also had to do a hill climb for the ages just to be able to not lose those final 20 seconds.  And that's when Roglic and the team panicked. There was no way that Roglic was going to ride like Pogacar was riding. It was too late. It showed in their bungled bike change. And it showed in how Roglic rode that final climb. Pogacar was riding smoothly and powerfully like a man possessed. He was going to win or he was going to blow. But he didn't blow. Roglic was riding scared and panicked. As the time checks kept coming in, he and his team saw the yellow jersey slipping away, but they were powerless. He just couldn't ride any faster. If Roglic hadn't panicked, he could possibly have matched Dumoulin's or Van Aert's times and finished somewhere between 2nd and 4th. That would've been a great time trial to finish eveny matched with Dumoulin. And even if he had, he STILL would have lost to Pogacar by over 20 seconds!  The only way Roglic would've kept yellow would be if he had ridden like Pogacar and rode all-out from the beginning without blowing up. But, that is only in hind-sight. And even if Roglic had gone all-out, does anybody think he have been able to match or come close to Pogacar's performance without blowing up? Nobody expected Pogacar to do it, so why would anybody expect Roglic to be able to do it, either? With 57 seconds in hand, nobody with the TT skill of Roglic would expect to ever need to have to ride that TT all-out. Nobody excepted Pogacar to ride that incredibly. So, as much as a number of people are saying that Roglic lost the yellow jersey with a bad performance, he really didn't. Pogacar won it with a performance for the ages.
