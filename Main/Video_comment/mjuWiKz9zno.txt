The high rake angle AND the 9 inch front axel trail should make the forks dive hard at low speeds. For example, a chopper with long forks (high rake angle) a slight lean at low speed makes the front tire dive a lot in the direction of the turn, making the bike want to turn even harder with a smaller turn radius. Had this been priced under a Goldwing through reasonable parts prices, we would have sold dozens of them. Most who saw it loved it, then recoiled in shock at the price.  For the first year it was surrounded by "Velvet Ropes".  After it sat on the dealer floor for a year without a buyer...it became just another motorcycle that we were paying interest on.   Soon, there was a salesman's bounty put on it and still....it sat un sold.  Asking someone to pay nearly double for a cruiser that did everything the VTX1800 did just didn't work.  We used it to draw people in, then show them what they could afford.     Eventually, the owner of the dealership bought it since he understood what this machine was.  A low volume, high quality, collectable machine.  It is probably still in his warehouse with less than 100 miles on it, waiting for the next owner to start it and then leave it dormant for years.
