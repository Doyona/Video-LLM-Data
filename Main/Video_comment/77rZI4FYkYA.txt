Hut glue grout hmm... I don't necessarily see that being a bad idea. In terms of materials you can definitely get some hot-glues that would be very hygenic, robust and flexible enough to not crack with age. Regular porous grout definitely sucks that's for damn sure. They are dirt magnets and kid of defeat the whole point of the tiles being so easy to keep clean in a wet-room.  As for making them perfectly smooth - that is easy. Just use a hot-air gun (or a few ideally). All hot-glues should be thermoplastic and re-meltable basically infinitely. You should be able to get them to flow nice and even quite easily after putting down the rough amount. Also - removing any superflous glue should be pretty easy on tiles if you just use a flat utility-blade. Especially if the glue is at a semi-malleable temperature it should cut through like butter.  The main downside I think would be time and tedium to do for a whole floor. A lot of melting, remelting and careful scraping to get it perfect. That's probably a big reason why grout is traditionally used. Once you know how to do that it's pretty simple.  But I do seriously wonder why silicone isn't used though. That would be much easier to apply - and is already used everywhere else in wetrooms for it's excellent properties.
