This album is paying homage, not ripping off the classic sounds of soul. The album is definitely short, but there's no filler. It doesn't strive ''modernize'' the sounds of soul, but I think they show other modern artists that great art can be made when you look back at where you came from. This album is definitely candy for the ears but it also made me think about how much I owe to the people in my past, to figures in history, in Black history, who live through every aspect of our culture today. I felt like dancing the whole time, I felt like celebrating and I was so grateful for the of legacy soul and R & B that moves me and consistently gets me through the day. But in this case I see and feel it differently. Silk Sonic expressed their individuality by truthfully showing reverence to those that came before them, by embracing collaboration and by letting themselves just have fun and feel joy fully, no cynicism.
