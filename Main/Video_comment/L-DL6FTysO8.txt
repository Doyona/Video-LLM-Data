The phenomenon you saw with the negative size is a side effect of backface culling. To put it simply, all faces on 3D models have normals, which is the way they're facing. A backface is the side of a face you see when the normal isn't facing you. Backface culling is used in a lot of games and prevents the rendering of backfaces, thus the weird opposite cube. This, of course, isn't really meant to be seen, but it's just the result of having a model with the normals facing inside and the backfaces facing outside. It's actually in virtually anything that has backface culling, from Blender (If you enable it) to Unity.
