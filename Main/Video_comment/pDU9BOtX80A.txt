For anyone wondering why the video is 4:3 instead of fully widescreen 16:9 like I was, it appears he is just trying to show the most accurate visual perspective. I didn't know this but GoPro (and most cameras) use a 4:3 sensor, it can only 'fake' widescreen by cutting off the top and bottom of the image and zooming in. So you're losing out on some quality and a lot visual information that could be important just to fill in the sides the screen. This isn't a big deal if you can actually see what you're filming and frame your shots, but when using a headmount it's not possible and the top/bottom of the image becomes more important; better to show more of the horizon and what you're walking towards as well as the ground.
