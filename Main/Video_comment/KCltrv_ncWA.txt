A tip from a lifelong construction guy....When sealing seams of any material outside or inside, ditch the silicone. The stuff just doesn't perform well compared to a polyurethane caulking like "Big Stretch" brands and others. Silicone just doesn't bond/stretch well over time with material temperature changes, especially when it's 2 different materials like metal to wood, or glass to wood. Every 3 years guaranteed you will be cutting silicone out and redoing it. Not so with polyurethane products. BTW, I built campground outhouses over 20 years ago in Idaho that you can still go to and that original poly caulking is still stable.....sealing glass board edges to cedar trim. That is impressive.
