I dont think you should critique that VC / dumb money that harshly.  Consider any startup that wants to take market share from established markets: the easiest and most attractive way is to be cheaper than your competitor. Established parties tend to have reduced costs to what they can bare already. This means you can gain some market share as a new business and survive among the other smaller companies. Later your company has to shift its business money as "dumb money" runs out, sure, but then you have a loyal customer base and variable customers. Especially since you dont have the benefits of size yet, which your established competitors definitely have and use against you. Ironically, capitalism becomes its own bottleneck.
