In Wales, UK, we have a pumped storage hydro power station called Dinorwig. For those who don't know, pumped storage stations work by having an upper and lower reservoir, separated vertically, by sometimes thousands of feet. In times of peak demand for electricity, water falls from the upper reservoir, through turbines, therefore creating power. When demand is light and electricity is cheap, the turbines are run in reverse thereby re-filling the upper reservoir.
