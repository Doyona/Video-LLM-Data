I don't know if they've considered this, but probably not... air conditioners don't generate cold, they just move heat. In the case of a fridge, the heat is moved from the inside cabinet to the grille on the back. In the case of these trains, they can surely only dump their excess heat into the air around them. That is, the stations. And the tubes themselves of course, but the piston effect will push all that air forward, since the tubes are narrow and don't hold a lot of air.   The other thing about air conditioning is that it's inefficient. It doesn't just move heat from the place you're cooling, the machinery itself creates heat. So overall you end up hotter than you started, if you take the whole system into accout.   I can't think of a way they could fix this. I suppose you could heat up water on board each train and dump it at stations or something, to take away the heat, but it's a bit impractical, and would cost a fair bit to create and run. Realistically it's gonna just dump the train's heat + the extra inefficiency heat, into the air in the stations. Making it WORSE!  A solution altogether might work if the stations cooperated, and mapped, then planned, the airflow between stations through the tunnels. Currently the piston effect is pretty useless pushing hot air from one hot place to another. If they could feed fresh air in, somewhere where they're able, they could let the trains move it round. Then once it's traveled far enough and got hot, dump it at some other station. Some giant vents for "in", some for "out". Powered mostly by the trains themselves, and actually SAVING money, because the trains will be pushing air that's free to get out of the way, rather than canned in the same tight tunnels, resisting their motion.
