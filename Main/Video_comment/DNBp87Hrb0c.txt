To sum, up Scientific Notation (the weird numbering sequence in the game), for anyone who doesn't know... the e[N] part of the number is basically how many decimals you need to move from the first number, to know what your actual number is. If it's a positive number you move your decimal right, if it's a negative number, you move it left. one quick example is: 1.56 e6 == 1,560,000 whereas 1.56 e-6 == 0.00000156  This is a simplified explanation to help us normal plebs (or people who didn't quite get to that part in their math-classes yet) to make sense of all the "powers of 10 math" that goes on behind the scenes.  It's a good way to make larger numbers easier to fit on a screen, that's for sure... and one possible way to prevent the integer-stack-overflows that spiff usually finds in his exploits.
