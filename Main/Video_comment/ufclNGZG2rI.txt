When I was a teenager my father's machine shop burned due to a fire that started in a adjacent unit.  My father had insurance and so did the neighbors unit where the fire started so it was a nightmare dealing with insurance companies.  Documentation is key to dealing with insurance and luckily you have plenty of video evidence documenting your assets.  Sorting through the ruins is also a important aspect of documenting your losses.  It seems like the area you are in has very lax rules pertaining to fire code and inspections.  There is many precautions to take to help prevent fires and limit the spread and damage potential.  When I picked a location for my shop I made sure it had fire sprinklers and a good fire alarm.  All of my flammable chemicals and liquids are stored in proper fire cabinets.  I limit the amount of fuel in the vehicles in my shop and I take careful precautions.  A rule I have at my shop is that someone must stay there for at least a hour after welding or plasma cutting.  I have electrical disconnects mounted on the wall by each piece of equipment and I shut them all off at the end of the day.  Another often overlooked thing is lithium batteries as they have the potential to spontaneously combust so all of my tool batteries and chargers are kept in a metal fire cabinet when they are being charged and not in use.  At my shop I have a very small 10x15 fenced in area behind my shop where I store waste oil, batteries and welding cylinders.  As a precaution I disconnect disconnect the batteries of all vehicles in my shop.  Even with all of the precautions in the world it is impossible to eliminate all fire risk so you do your best to mitigate the risk as much as possible.  I am extremely cautious due to seeing what my father went through.  The best way to look at this situation is that you will probably be much better off after rebuilding and use this fire as a motivating factor to make the next step and grow the business.  The most important thing of all is having good iron clad insurance and documentation.
