For me, astronauts are professionals who work and live on orbit. People who go to space or deep space with a mission, to conduct science or other valuable work in support of that. These people have dedicated much of their lives to this profession, had countless years of study and training, and do things that only a relatively small group of incredibly skilled people in the world can do. This New Shepard launch is just another quick hop out of the atmosphere and back. If you ask me, there should come a clear distinction between professional space travelers or astronauts (people who go to the ISS and conduct science and maintenance) and tourists or hobbyists who pay big money for their own story of a lifetime.
