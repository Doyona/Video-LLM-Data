The best way to peel garlic is actually to do this:   Separate it down to the individual clove, then set it on a solid surface. Like on the countertop other a wooden cutting board. Take a large, chopping knife and lay ***ONE FLAT SIDE*** over a single clove and firmly hit ***THE OTHER FLAT SIDE*** with your palm. Do not chop it with the blade part of the knife, and only hit it hard enough to break off the skin. You do not want to crush the garlic or it will change its flavor.   Some people familiar with "Mythical Kitchen" have probably seen Josh palm heel strike garlic a lot, it's similar to that but his garlic is already peeled so he's just crushing it to cook it. But I don't know if you can get pre-peeled garlic in Germany.   Other method you can do if you're going to be cooking a whole head of garlic is this:   Cut off the top of the head with a sharp knife to expose all the cloves but leave the skin on. Wrap it in tinfoil but leave the cut part exposed so you can drizzle oil inside. Once the oil has been drizzled onto the cloves, close the tinfoil ball and bake it. You can then just squeeze the cloves out of the head with tongs.   Also when you make garlic confit with whole cloves you can keep it in the refrigerator for as long as 2 months as long as your store it properly. To store garlic confit:   Make sure you keep it in a glass jar with an airtight lid. After you finish baking it, let it come down to room temperature before refrigerating it. Let it sit on the counter in the jar until it stops steaming, then cover it loosely until it's room temperature. Once it's room temperature it can be covered fully and kept in the fridge for up to 2 months.
