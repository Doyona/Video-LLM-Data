This is important if you are having an issue with a piece of equipment but have not figured out the cause yet. I look around you brilliant stock of farming equipment and the risk of fire can be overwhelming so this is it in a nutshell. For fire to break out you need three things so consider it a triangle, you need oxygen you need a source of heat and you need fuel. Break that triangle take away anyone of those three and you are safe. So oxygen, its impossible to remove in that shed so you are left with the other two,  a source of heat or fuel. Don't store any fuel inside farm building that can vaporize, just remember petrol vapours can travel many yards along the ground and then find an exposed flame and ignite. fuel can also be anything, your grain, straw anything combustible like that gives you two out of the three components in that fire triangle I mentioned. A hot exhaust on your tractors after a day out working could potentially be that third ingredient needed to start a fire so leave said vehicles outside in the open air until the are fully cooled before putting them to bed at night.  You might want to consider contacting your local fire department and ask them for advice give them the guided tour of your business and I'm sure they will be there to lend a hand. They don't pay for replacement equipment; they pay the _depreciated value_ of the lost equipment, often on their timescale.
