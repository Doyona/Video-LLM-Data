Back to basics. The mobile hype is over. Early mistakes have been made, the easy solutions attempted.  Maybe this is the beginning of the 2nd wave? Instead of dumbing down the functionalities and mechanics of a game to make it lend itself to mobile, perhaps now we will (thanks to superior computing power of mobile devices and other breakthroughs) manage to create completely new, novel, mobile-first iterations of truly amazing games, while also pushing the envelope on the tried-and-true desktop/console form-factors.  I always found Rome 1's mechanics to be far deeper and more meaningful than say, Rome 2. Some say this was the engine upgrade, in my view it was a sign of something deeper. If we think back to the 2010's and after the Financial Crisis, mobile hype was raging and virtually every company had to at least on some level seriously consider the coming mobile revolution and their positioning in it. In my view the "dumbing down" of mechanics was in part to future-proof IPs for possible mobile spin-offs.  Here we are, a decade later, and a certain virus has taught us the value of having a good rig. Mobile is great, but it cannot be a substitute for PC/console. It must stand on its own two legs and, for it to truly change the world as we think it should (or will), will necessitate mobile-native development that doesn't replace the PC/desktop, but either augments it or is completely independent of it. This means, in other words, that PC/desktop developer units won't have to try and cater to two kinds of ecosystem at once.
