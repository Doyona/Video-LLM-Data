This was awesome, a couple of tips that may help... 1. If you are using a light box secure the base and top papers to it using washi tape, it will hold your art in place and avoid the dreaded slip. 2. There's a site called croquis cafe. Com  (you want the model index) that has a huge mass of models of different shapes and sizes in different poses, please note its for life drawing so they are in their birthday's units for the most part) it's awesome for seeing how the body looks in different angles and isn't as generic as some apps can be, they also do live drawing sessions with times poses on vinmo. It's all free unless you fancy supporting them with a donation. I highly recommend them. (you can find some of their images on Instagram but they are censored). My final tip is 3. Look on secondhand book shops for artist reference books or  photography and fashion books. I use awesomebooks. Com which ships worldwide, you can find some really good references for practicing and if you own the book you can use it to trace onto a scrap piece of copy paper your starting shapes. If you own a tablet and spot a image you want to use personally or adapt into your artwork screenshot it then load it into a drawing app and trace the shapes using that. This will give you a bit more confidence when it comes to drawing your figure. I recommend old books as they cost approx Â£3 each approx $4. And are delivered straight to your door for a tiny fee. (awesomebooks delivers free in the uk and very low rate to other countries). I have built up a huge reference from animals, plants, fashion, shoes, antiques, architecture. Yoga poses (which are great for pushing your anatomy drawing!) .. For not a lot of money I hope these may help someone, the most important thing is though that if your drawing outside of your comfort zone is never to be harsh on yourself, if its not looking right look closer where its good and isolate the mistakes and be proud of your achievements so far, by doing this you can narrow down your weaknesses and work on them. Never see your art as a failure only as a step forwards to creating something amazing.
