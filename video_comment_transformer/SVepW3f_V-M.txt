The answer to the question, do high voltage fuses contain special gases. Is yes and no. Some fuses do contain special gases, and some don't. I would suspect that the fuses inside inexpensive microwaves likely don't contain any special gases. Often the conductors inside of the fuse bodies are specially formulated so that when they vaporize they create special gases that have an arc quenching effect, but they are not all built that way. Fuses are made with many voltage, current, energy and time ratings, so you have understand the circuit that you are trying to protect, before you can specify the right fuse. Like you said; only replace opened fuses with new fuses with the same ratings.
