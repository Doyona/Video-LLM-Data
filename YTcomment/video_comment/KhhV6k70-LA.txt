Nice video; I'm a bit confused about raised garden boxes. These started out in inner city areas like rooftops or paved courtyards because of a lack of planting space and usually a lack of soil on a skyscraper roof. Then after being reported on in various women's magazine's, every woman in suburbia had to have a least one raised garden box, usually standing them up on top of...soil, in their yard, where there is sufficient amounts of good ole dirt, and sunshine. So...my thought goes to, what's the point? People are spending significant money and time to build a box to hold dirt...over the top of dirt, not a rooftop or concrete courtyard. There is no benefit to put a wood box of dirt over a yard full of dirt. A raised garden box will kill the grass under it regardless, so why not just till the yard into a garden, adding mulch and good soil, forgo the nice (expensive) wood box, save a tree and plant your crop. Bugs and dogs will still be an issue in a raised garden box. Just food for thought.
