Btw, just a little suggestion: don't rely heavily on the sweet soy sauce. That's be like masking the actual flavor of nasi goreng, which is usually flavorful and spicy. You won't taste the actual flavor if you put too much sweet soy sauce, that's why the nasi goreng kampung exists as the contrary version (also several versions that only use little amount of sweet soy sauce).  The keys for great nasi goreng are: 1. The rice; the leftover would be the best. Also, don't cook the rice using too much water, it shouldn't be sticky and should easily "breakable".  2. High heat.
