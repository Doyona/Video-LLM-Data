Warning. Warning. Warning. Different brands of LED tubes are wired differently.  There is no industry standard.   For the one he is showing you 110VAC is applied to the two pins at one end of the tube.  The pins are the other end are dummies.  For another brand 110VAC is applied from one end to the other end.  So one end has the pins tied together and you can put your hot lead there on either pin. At the other end the two pins are also tied together and you can wire your neutral to either one.  If you wire hot and neutral to the two pins at one end you will create a short circuit and hopefully pop the circuit breaker.  And unhopefully burn down your house.    Some tubes brag about being compatible with your existing ballast.  If you are light weight enough about wiring up electrical lamps that you feel like you want to leave your existing ballast in place and power the tubes that way then please just keep using fluorescent tubes and don't change to LED.    This is a sad state of affairs that tubes are wired differently.  This means you have a nice LED fixture and years from now the LED tube goes out.  So you go to the store and buy another LED tube.  You plug it in, flip on the switch and POW!    Another problem is every LED tube I have tried puts out a great deal of RF noise in the longwave band.  Turn on your LED tubes and every AM radio in your neighborhood turns to static.  That's why I bought a lifetime supply of old fashioned ballasts and fluorescent tubes and will not switch to LED until they reduce RF noise emitted from them by at least 80dB.
