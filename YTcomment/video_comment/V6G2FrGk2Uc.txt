Here are the problems with your process: 1. You're using what looks like a pine 6x6. I'm saying this because I've noticed that pine sometimes has a greenish tinge to it. This doesn't work out well plain or with a light stain. If you intended to do what you did by staining it dark then there's no problem. It's not very versatile otherwise. 2. You haven't recessed the bolts completely. I personally wouldn't have used bolts. Basic screws or even nails would've been better. For greater aesthetic appeal it's not a noticeable reduction in strength for purely functional purposes. 3. Your stain application is all wrong. When dealing with softwoods in particular, ALWAYS condition your wood. I even go a step further and use a pore filler. If you don't condition, your stain will come out blotchy or too dark. 4. Avoid red stains when working with woods like pine. Red stain works on hardwoods that can take a good polish and have a deep grain. Pine has, in my experience, never taken a good polish. Only if you use chemical sealing and finishing compounds have I ever seen pine shine and it doesn't look too good. Artificial. When woring with pine, stick to light and dark brown shades. This can make it look like oak which just has a warmer feel.
