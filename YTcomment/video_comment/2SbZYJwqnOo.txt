Being classically trained, we were taught that you never butter or oil a pan when making a sponge cake.  True sponge cakes do not use any sort of chemical agents to help with the leavening, the egg does it all, and the egg MUST have something to grab onto, in order to climb, if it encounters a slippery side of a baking pan like oil, or butter it has nothing to grab onto, and so it will not rise as effectively.  This was the way I was taught, so I thought I should provide correct info.
