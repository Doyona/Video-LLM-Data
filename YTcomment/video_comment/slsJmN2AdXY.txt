Great to see a clueful owner using ISO containers in a PRACTICAL manner as modules!  If you build a container structure this is one to copy.   I would use High Cube spec containers for the considerable internal height difference with the same footprint and would paint the roof if not the whole structure in white industrial (NOT big-box hardware store) roof coating for energy efficiency. I did so with my 2x High Cube container shop and it worked very well.   If you want to blend with your surroundings,, coat the sides as you like but ensure your roof is coated with a product which resists "ponding" and consult with an industrial roof contractor or study contracting forums. I suggest not beginning with a 20' container unless you are forced to by lot space constraints as the price is typically within several hundred dollars of a forty-foot High Cube. Incidentally, some camouflage patterns are visually interesting and can make your container vanish behind trees and shrubs. Camo done right works with the landscape and doesn't look like some military artifact (though some people like that, I just wanted to avoid the usual "some asshole dumped a sea can in their yard" result. Barn paint schemes work well if you live in a suitable area. Paint can give any attractive illusion you want and the best way to thickly coat a container is by brush and roller.   A container is a great structural improvement over typical single-wide mobile homes. They are good to over 100mph wind speeds unanchored. I suggest having a power pole with a panel and outdoor outlets placed before you start construction so you will have very convenient site power during your build. I did that then connected my shop via a typical weatherhead arrangement. You can ground your welding machine to the shell then run your stinger where you like. If connecting two ISOs a suitcase feeder is handy. You or your weldor can usually rent any needed equipment but buying it is trivial compared to construction cost and you'll have the gear for life.  If you are not familiar with containers, build yourself a shop first! You get an immediately usable shop with tool and material storage, which is why containers are so often used on job sites.
