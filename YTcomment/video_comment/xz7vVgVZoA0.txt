Love the videos my friend! Try elephant garlic if it can withstand your hardiness zone. The bulbs are big enough you can let them go to flower, and  you'll still get decent size bulbs. That way your bees can enjoy them too, and they open blooms in June July when fall planted. If your winter is too cold and you have to spring plant, it may take cloves a year to grow to large rounds, before reaching blooming size with a spring planting schedule. It may also affect bloom time. After the bees finish with the flowers, you have a small window of opportunity to harvest the blossoms, which can be used in cooking like chive blossoms for extra flavor and color in various dishes.
