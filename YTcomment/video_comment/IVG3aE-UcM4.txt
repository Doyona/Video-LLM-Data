There is a better technique in my opinion that probably would have been a lot cleaner and dryer. Especially for carpeted basements next to or by your sump pump that your replacing. If your not as experienced as the guy in this video, and you loosen the top of the check valve first you run the risk of having water go everywhere. You just have to loosen and remove the PVC piping from the bottom of the check valve first as opposed to the top (as shown in this video). Then stick a screwdriver up through the bottom of that check valve releasing all the actual valve. Just make sure that the check valve your about to release is over your drain before placing the screwdriver up there. After draining all the water out of the above PVC pipe you can then remove the entire check valve without water going everywhere. Please just respond if you have any questions about this technique.
