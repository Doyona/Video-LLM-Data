We have a really lousy selection of Jamaican rum here in Canada. Right now, I don't have _any_ Jamaican rum in the house, but I do have this Plantation rum from Fiji, which to my surprise is full of Jamaican funk. So I thought I'd improvise, and try a 3 rum base using Patron Cafe for the liqueur, which I'm currently enjoying as I type this. Normally, I make these using Appleton Special Reserve, white Bacardi, and Kahlua, and they're pretty good, but the one I'm having now is just soooo much better: 1.5 oz Fiji Plantation rum (funk) 0.5 oz Goslings Black Seal (dark) 0.5 oz white Bacardi (because) 0.75 oz Patron Cafe  (warning: the dark coffee flavour of this is much more powerful than Kahlua, so cut it back if you don't want a strong coffee note with lingering aftertaste) 0.75 oz Lemon (Personal preference. Stick to a whole ounce if that's what you like) 0.5 oz simple syrup 1.5 oz pineapple  I might try skipping the Bacardi altogether with the next one, and use a full ounce of Goslings instead. The Patron Cafe could easily be cut back to 0.5 oz, but I like the boldness the way it is. It starts with the tang of fruit, quickly develops into dark coffee, and leaves a coffee aftertaste. Perhaps similar if one replaced the simple syrup with more Kahlua in search of a stronger coffee note.
I used Kahlua Especial (75 proof version). If you do use Kahlua - I recommend dropping that coffee liqueur to 0.5 oz. I used black seal and a 3 year light for the other components.
