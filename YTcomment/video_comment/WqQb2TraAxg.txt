New fabric & clothing are treated with "preservatives", like formaldehyde, to prevent molding etc. I have some experience with eco dying & printing, natural dying, and lots of tea & coffee staining/dying of both paper and fabric. You really should scour (boil) your new fabrics. Or at least run them through a hot wash in the machine, with mild/little soap. Some folks use a little "washing soda". No fabric softeners or anything else... in the dryer, too. Even used or thrifted fabrics should be washed of any oils, detergents, etc.  The scour/hot wash not only removes the residues, it helps to open the weave & fibers, making them more receptive to the tannins & dyes/stains. The staining will be imbedded deeper & last longer. I know it's a pain. Especially when we just want to get to makin'!!üòÅ But it can be worth the extra effort.
