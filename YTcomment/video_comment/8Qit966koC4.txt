SCIENCE! The maillard effect is when you sear the outside of the steak and the amino acids and sugars make that delicious brown crust. That sear does not in fact trap in any discernable amount of moisture, as steam/moisture can still pass through the sear as science has proven now, but you still want the delicious maillard effect from the sear. SO! Even cooking at a lower temp, then finished with a sear is the way to go. That's why so many chefs and food scientists recommend sous vide plus sear. However, that's expensive and time consuming. What the method in this video shows is a great alternative! The constant flipping causes fantastic evenness because the sides never have that long to heat up or cool down by a large amount, but you're simultaneously getting the maillard effect sear too. Flipping does not release moisture, science has proven that untrue as well. It actually makes your steak cook up to 30% faster! It also lessens curling/cupping when connective tissue cooks faster and shrinks up.

Cool trick to gauge "well-ness" of your steak while cooking: touch the tips of your thumb and index finger (don't squeeze or pinch, just touch). Now, poke the fleshy base of your thumb with your other index finger. That's about as squishy as a rare steak. Touching your thumb to your middle finger gives you a medium rare, ring finger gives you medium, and your pinky gives you about medium-well/well done. Not an exact science but it gives you a rough idea of where you are while you're cooking.
