Using charcoal filters and going though a filter a week?  In the aquarium hobby we call these carbon filters and they are mostly a scam. I have four tanks and none of them use carbon/charcoal at all. What you actually need in your filters is mechanical filter media (a sponge or filter wool) to capture particles in the water and biological media (ceramic rings, or bio balls etc, a big enough sponge will also work for biological) to host beneficial bacteria, which converts ammonia to nitrite and nitrite to nitrate, which is much less toxic to fish. This filter media can be cleaned and the sponge and biological media never needs replacing (filter wool will start to fall apart and need replacing after some months but is very cheep)  Carbon/charcoal is not mechanical or biological filtration, its chemical filtration, most people only use this after medicating their fish or if there is some other need to remove chemicals from the water. Fish shops love carbon filters because they need replacing every 2 weeks which means lots of money for them, this is also why lots of new fish tanks come with carbon filters instead of what they actually need.  In your case the gravel/grow media in your grow beds will act as more then enough biological media. You may still want some kind of mechanical filtration if you don't want your grow beds to clog up to quickly. The real benefit of the plants is that they eat the nitrate which will massively reduce how often you need to do water changes.
