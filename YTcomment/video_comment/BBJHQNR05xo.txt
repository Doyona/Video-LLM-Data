Thanks, good explanation of those "mystery" cutouts and notches.  One point that perhaps should be clarified: "pitch" of a roof is NOT the same as rise/run.  Pitch is defined as rise/span (from one outside edge of the top plate to the opposite outside edge for standard gable roofs).  For example: a roof pitch of 1/2 is equal to a rise/run of 12in/12in; 1/3 pith equals a rise/run of 8in/12in; etc.  "Pitch" may have become an old fashioned term, but I still encounter it and using it incorrectly could confuse 'newbies' causing them to misinterpret plans they try to read.

Pitch is the ratio of the total rise(height at the peak) to the span of the building. So, if the building is 24 feet wide and the rise of the roof is 6 inches in 12 inches(giving a total rise of 6 feet) the roof is a 1/4 pitch, because 6' is 1/4 of the 24' span. If the rise were 12 inches in 12(giving a rise of 12') the roof would be a 1/2 pitch. Forgive me for stating something you already know. But for those of us just learning about roof angles its good to have that understanding.
