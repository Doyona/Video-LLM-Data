good video, but the chemistry is a little off.  you've sprayed a finite amount of HCl on your concrete.  The HCl reacts with the lime in the concrete.  Once the total mass of HCl has reacted with the available lime (Calcium carbonate), there isn't a lot of point in pouring an alkaline on it to stop the reaction, because the reaction has already stopped due to there being no more acid.  That would be like pouring water on a fire that had already burned itself out.  The time to use the alkaline is while the acid is still reacting with the lime, if you actually want to disrupt that (maybe you didn't dilute your acid as much as you thought, and the concrete is getting darker than you wanted it to get).
