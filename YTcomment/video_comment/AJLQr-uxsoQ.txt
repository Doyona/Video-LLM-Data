Here's the question.............Why?
If you want a smoother hole, I would suggest that you make a cofferdam of wax or putty around the spot where you want the hole, put a slurry of water and carborundum (say 120 grit) into the cofferdam, chuck a soft metal rod or tube (say cooper or brass or aluminum) of the diameter that you want your hole into your drill press, and then repeatedly press down and lift up this rod or tube  as if it were a drill bit or hole saw. This is what folks like to make their own Cassegrain telescope mirrors used to do and although it takes a bit of time, it works just fine. (I haven't done this myself, but I have done something similar, namely cutting through Pyrex glass tubing using a piece of aluminum mounted in a hack saw frame and a carborundum slurry.)
