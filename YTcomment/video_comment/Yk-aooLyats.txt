Further to my comments re constructing jewellery boxes finished with sui su ban process.  I‚Äôve made 2.  The first - brushing before losing all burn char leaving a natural brown on the pith i.e. Early Wood, and a Dk brown on the hard grain I.e. Late wood.   The 2nd - brushed enough to return to natural wood colour, stained red,  Both finished with water based clear lacquer.  I eliminated the warping effect of the heat by dovetail construction of the box at corners and tongue & groove fitting of the plain bottom and the top ( lid panel of 19 mm/3/4 in  and raised, clearing top edges of ends and sides for brushing clearance. The double thickness to resist warping of this wide panel.  The lid was cut from the box before dyeing and clear topcoat.  Hinges and catch attached lastly.   The only pine available to me was a mix of Locally grown Monterey (Pinus radiate ). And  New Zealand grown Oregon/Douglas Fir.  The latter growing faster in NZ than USA and a lower strength and hardness(S 7) which makes it easier to brush out.  It is pink and the other pine whiter and effects the appearance of the dye.
