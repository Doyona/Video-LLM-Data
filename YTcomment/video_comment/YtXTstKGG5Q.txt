Good tutorial but your are mis-informed about anti-foaming agents in detergent oils.  Virtually ALL SAE Service graded oils for modern cars have anti-foaming agents as one of their PRIMARY ingredients.  Detergent is in oil to help keep carbon and oil oxidation components suspended in the oil rather than stick to the inside of the engine.  Anti-foaming agents are in automotive oils because "foamy oil is a poor lubricant".   Any time you pull a dipstick on an engine and you see more than 2 bubbles in the oil on it, you need to change the oil soon.  Immediately if it is a VVT, overhead cam, or high performance engine.  Foamy oil in close tolerance Babbitt bearing surfaces (rods, mains, cam bearings) is a recipe for ruin.
