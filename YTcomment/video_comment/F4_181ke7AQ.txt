your marriage visual is cute and i actually thought it was so pretty seeing it in the three colors.   when i was a brand new crocheter, i would put an extra stitch into that gap.  The visual cues that helped me correct that were two fold:  1. that extra space is where your starting chain is coming out of.  that starting chain won't be coming out of the last actual stitch so, if the starting chain is "inside" that last "loop" then you don't add a stitch there.   2. Not much later, i got more comfy with the anatomy of the stitch and realized that if you follow the post of the stitch up, the little "hole" space to the top right is the top of that stitch, NOT the space on the left side (assuming you are crocheting right handed of course).  So identifying the post of the last stitch in the previous round and following it up and to the right to find the top of that stitch tells you where to put your last stitch.   That second identifier is what keeps you from making mistakes when working cables and post stitches.   A lot of times a post stitch can "hide" the top of the next stitch a bit,  making it easy to skip the next non post stitch but if you always identify the post and know where the top of the stitch is in relation to that post, you'll know not to miss it.   This is also useful when working corners on granny squares when you might accidentally cover the top of the first stitch after a corner but, seeing that first post tells you that there's a stitch there so you know to find it, even if it's buried.
