I like the double fry and freezing. Both are the best for crispy fries, the first fry precooks and draws moisture out then freezing draws more moisture from the fry to the outside where it is evaporated off in the second fry. Here is what I use for Mcdonalds French Fries   The way I get crispy fries similar to Mcdonalds: 1) Cut them thin and soak them first in cold water for at least an hour up to 4 hours to get the starch out, ice water is best. You can tell they are good when the water runs clear when you rinse them  2)While they are soaking the last bit bring a pot of water to a boil with some white sugar and corn syrup in it  3)After they have soaked in the ice water turn OFF the boiling sugar water and transfer your fries into that for 5 minutes to pre cook and give them that extra flavour most people are missing in their home fries.  4)After 5 minutes in the shut off water take them out and pat them dry, at this point you can freeze them for later use or cook them right away. (I always do a ten pound bag of Russets all at once and freeze the leftovers for later use then make more after those run out.) 5) To COOK them you need to do 2 SEPARATE deep fries this is essential for crispy fries at home, the ones Mcdonalds gets in the store have had all of this done to them up until that point so they only need 1 cook in store 6)2 minutes in 350-375 oil (I have a little deep fryer so I leave it on 375 to try and keep the temp up) then take them out and let them cool until they are cool to the touch, it is beneficial to pat off the oil and moisture that seeps out as this point but a lot of the time I don't for the mess and to save paper towel. 7) After they have cooled back into the same temp oil for 5-7 minutes depending how thick you cut them   Some tips for the best fries:   1)Russets are best 2)Cut them thinner than you think you need to if you like them like Mcdonalds/thin, I always used to think they will be too thin but you figure it out after a few times 3)If they are not staying crispy after you cook them that is probably because your oil is too old/dirty. NEW oil is best for crispiness and USED oil adds more flavour to your fries, the BEST fries at a Mcdonalds are not right after they change the oil but they are the crispiest. The BEST fries are about 1-2 days after its been changed depending how busy it's been, they are still crispy but have more flavor.   Good luck if you try it let me know

People mention dextrose. That is used when fries are frozen so that they will easily separate. The double frying or cooking is to reduce the moisture. The less moisture you have in the fries befre they go into the oil the less the heat of the oil will drop and the fries suck up the oil. The thicker are your fries the longer it will take to brown and thus cook. You want to get the oil up to the righttemp, rop the fries into it in amounts that do not drop the temp too much, get the empt back up, let then fry then take out and de-grease. Always wait a few minutes between fring batchs to get oil temp back up..
Forget about peeling if you wash the potatoes very well such as wetting let stand a few minutes then scrip 3 times. Then towel dry the outside, cut into whatever size you want and then with a hair dryer air dry them in a willow basket so that the air can circulate and pass through. The more dry heated air you put through them the dryer they will get on the outside and of course inner moisture will rise to the exterior. I air dry them while the oil is heating. When the fries are sort of like a cloudy white on the exterior I get the thickest one to drop into the heating oil to test the hotness of the oil. When the oil is so hot that when a fry is dropped into it and comes to a float within a few minutes then you know it is hot enough to start dropping in the thickest ones so that they have the longest to cook and do not reduce the heat too much. You want the oil to be cracking not girgling because girbling is steaming them and they are just boiling instead of frying. For one serving (which is generous) I cut 2 potatoes that are about the length of the hand of the intended. And they cook up in about 10 minutes. Put a couple screens over the top to catch oily air. When you remove from thre oil I put them on a couple layers of paper toweling and place a paper towel in each hand then run them through the pile to immediately get off surface oil. Do not throw out the used paper towels as they can be used to start fireplaces or BBQ's. The potates while air drying will not turn color but do not leave them alone without air passing over them as then they will turn grey.
