In the south with intense heat virtually year round, if you put the nails below the tar line it will leak profusely. Flour sifter would have fewer leaks. Here? Always on or above the tar line. If you nail on the tar line the heat, in the south will stick the nail without rise later on causing "fish mouth" from the nail pushing up as the shingles swells slightly from heat. If you go above the tar line with the nail you get a full contact tar stick in the heat from the covering shingle. In the winter, nailing above the tar line will be fine because the shingles is not swollen from heat. If the covering shingle does not stick good however, in the following summer the rise of heated shingles can possibly cause a nail to rise pushing an open curve to the covering shingle.
