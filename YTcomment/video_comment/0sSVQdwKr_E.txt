You know how they say that for these stovetop models, you're not supposed to use really fine grinds (or else they'll go through the filter)?  Well, I have a lot of time on my hands... so what I do is alternate between 3 layers of coffee grinds in the basket:  So, from bottom to top, here's what it looks like: 5-10% regular/medium-fine, 80-85% fine, 5% regular grind.  I do use a burr mill to get the regular to get the regular-medium fine grinds for the bottom and top layers...... but then I break the rules and use a blade-equipped spice grinder as a second stage to grind the burr-milled grinds even further to get the fine/extra-fine grinds.  I only pulse it for no more than 10-15 seconds... just until it looks like damp sand (I think that's the key).  What this does, in my opinion, is that it creates not only extra pressure inside the basket, but it keeps the fine grinds up and off of the basket's filter screen and at the same time, away from the top filter screen of the upper reservoir.  Also, while many suggest that you are not supposed to tamper your coffee in these models, you actually can, but don't do it too hard or else it'll just be too dense for anything to pass through, no matter what type of layering scheme you use.
