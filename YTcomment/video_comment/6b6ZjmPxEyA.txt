Actually, you shouldn't use a propane torch to heat the surface of the granite - there's always a percentage of residual propane that will never burn (hydrocarbons) and that will leave a slight amount of non-visible oil on the surface. A heat gun or high wattage hair dryer is the best alternative. Or, place the granite in a oven at 150-200 degrees Fahrenheit for 10 minutes. The same idea when painting metal - an auto body shop will never use a propane torch just prior to painting. If you only have a torch then wipe the surface with a non-petroleum solvent (alcohol or acetone) just prior to applying your project or finish.
