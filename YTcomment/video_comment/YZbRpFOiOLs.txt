Laura, I think you should learn a bit more about fermentation and bread.  I know of no artisan baker that makes baguettes in such a short period of time.  Most require about 24 hours in order to ensure proper fermentation of the dough.  This usually begins with a poolish or a levain. Secondly, shaping is extremely important in all bread making, especially the baguette with its tapered ends and fatter middle.  In addition to this, seam side is always down when baking.  Lastly, slash your bread right before going into the oven  This will help create the bread "ear" and beautiful split that you are looking for. As with your baguettes, there is no color variation on the top due to slashing them too early.    I will applaud you for your encouragement of keeping the dough wet for this recipe.  Also, I'm glad you introduced the very important concept of steam when baking bread.
